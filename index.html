Technology strategy for R&D that expands what scientists can do

In architecture, a pendentive is the structural innovation that allows a circular dome to rest on a square base—a smooth transition between incompatible geometries. In R&D, the challenge is similar: laboratory methods, data systems, manufacturing processes, and AI models each have their own logic, and making them work together requires more than technical integration.

We help organizations invest in technology that genuinely expands human capability—not just automation that substitutes for it.

Philosophy

Most technology investments in R&D are evaluated by what they automate: How many experiments can run unattended? How much human labor can we remove?

We ask a different question: What can scientists do now that they couldn't do before?

The distinction matters. Technologies that merely substitute for human effort often create what economists call "so-so automation"—systems that displace workers without generating commensurate new value. The best investments create genuine usefulness: they extend reach into new experimental territory, surface patterns humans couldn't perceive, make distributed expertise accessible, amplify judgment under complexity, and help reasoning accumulate rather than dissipate.

We help clients distinguish between these possibilities and invest in the latter.
